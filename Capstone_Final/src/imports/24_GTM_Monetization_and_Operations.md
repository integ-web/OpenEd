# OpenEd GTM, Monetization, and Operations Document

## Positioning

OpenEd is a free AI-native learning platform for serious courses. It helps learners learn actively and prove capability through sources, tutor support, practice, artifacts, rubrics, and portfolios.

## Who to launch with

Start narrow:

- AI learners.
- Product/tech learners.
- Independent educators.
- Serious self-learners.
- Communities interested in frontier AI and applied AI.

Do not start as a broad marketplace.

## Launch strategy

### Phase 1: FME flagship beta

- Invite 30-100 learners.
- Measure completion, tutor usage, artifact creation.
- Collect feedback.
- Fix UX and dark theme.

### Phase 2: Educator alpha

- Invite 5-10 educators.
- Ask them to create one module or mini-course.
- Test course QA and Studio friction.

### Phase 3: Public beta

- Open FME and 1-2 additional courses.
- Enable public portfolios.
- Publish course quality standards.

### Phase 4: Community growth

- Add learning challenges.
- Add public showcase of artifacts.
- Add educator applications.

## Monetization while learners stay free

Learners should access learning content for free.

Potential revenue streams:

| Stream | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Educator Pro | Later | Advanced course analytics, AI-assisted building, cohort tools. |
| Managed AI credits | Later | Learners can BYOK for free; OpenEd can offer optional managed AI. |
| Verified assessment | Later | Optional paid/human-reviewed proof, not basic learning. |
| Sponsored courses | Later | Companies/communities sponsor free access. |
| Institution portals | Much later | Colleges/organizations pay for cohorts/admin/analytics. |
| Enterprise learning | Much later | Private courses, SSO, reporting. |

## Operational model

### OpenEd Team roles

- Product/UX.
- Engineering.
- Course QA reviewer.
- Trust/safety reviewer.
- Educator success.
- Support/moderation.
- Growth/community.

### Early operations cadence

Weekly:

- Product metrics review.
- Tutor failure review.
- Course QA review.
- User feedback triage.

Monthly:

- Educator onboarding review.
- Market scan.
- Risk review.
- Accessibility QA.

## Supply strategy

V1 course supply should be curated.

Course acceptance criteria:

- Clear learner audience.
- Strong sources.
- Real practice.
- Artifact output.
- Rubrics.
- Tutor context.
- No unsupported claims.

## Demand strategy

Demand should come from:

- Strong flagship course.
- Public build-in-progress updates.
- Learner proof examples.
- Educator case studies.
- Communities around AI, product, coding, and learning.

## Investor narrative

OpenEd is not a content marketplace. It is a learning/proof infrastructure company.

Early product wedge:

- AI-native course runtime.
- Free learner access.
- BYOK AI tutoring.
- Educator quality tooling.
- SkillProof portfolios.

Long-term opportunity:

- Source-mapped course graph.
- Tutor pedagogy engine.
- Proof/skill graph.
- Educator ecosystem.
- Institution/organization layer.

## Operational risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Free model has no revenue | Delay heavy AI costs through BYOK; monetize educator/institution later. |
| Course supply is low | Start curated and high-quality, not broad. |
| Educators resist QA | Position QA as trust badge and course improvement. |
| Learners want certificates only | Make portfolios and proof more valuable than passive certificates. |
| AI cost surprises | Usage limits, BYOK, caching, managed AI pricing later. |

## First 90-day plan

1. Freeze OpenEd v1 scope.
2. Rebrand/refactor FME into OpenEd course runtime.
3. Implement auth/IAM.
4. Improve design system.
5. Launch learner beta.
6. Validate tutor/artifact loop.
7. Build Educator Studio v0.
8. Draft first course QA policy.
9. Create public waitlist for learners and educators.
