# OpenEd Impact and What We Are Fixing in EdTech

## Purpose

This document defines the impact thesis. OpenEd is not just a nicer course UI. It is an attempt to fix structural weaknesses in online learning.

## What current edtech often gets wrong

### 1. Passive learning

Many courses are videos with quizzes attached. Learners watch, pause, forget, and move on.

OpenEd fix:

- Active lesson workspace.
- Practice tasks.
- AI tutor.
- Artifacts.
- Feedback.

### 2. Weak doubt-solving

Learners often get stuck and leave. Paid tutors or communities are not always accessible.

OpenEd fix:

- AI tutor in every lesson.
- Prompt chips for common doubts.
- Source-grounded answers.
- Escalation/reporting.

### 3. No trust map

Courses rarely show where claims come from. Learners do not know what is required and what is advanced.

OpenEd fix:

- Source cards.
- Required vs optional labels.
- Difficulty tags.
- Lesson-source mapping.

### 4. Weak assessment

Quizzes are often too easy; exams can be too heavy or external. Certificates become weak signals.

OpenEd fix:

- Proof ladder.
- Practice tasks.
- Artifacts.
- Rubrics.
- Revision history.
- SkillProof portfolios.

### 5. Educator quality is unclear

Some platforms require famous institutions; others allow anyone to upload low-quality courses.

OpenEd fix:

- Educator Studio.
- Publish-readiness QA.
- Source mapping.
- Rubrics.
- OpenEd Team review.

### 6. AI is bolted on

Many platforms add chatbots but do not redesign learning.

OpenEd fix:

- Tutor integrated into lesson context, sources, practice, artifacts, and rubrics.

### 7. Learners pay before proof

Many platforms charge before learners know if the course fits.

OpenEd fix:

- Free learner access.
- BYOK/optional managed AI later.
- Portfolio-based outcome.

## Impact goals

### Learner impact

- Improve course completion.
- Improve self-efficacy.
- Increase practice and artifact creation.
- Make advanced learning paths clearer.
- Give learners portable proof.

### Educator impact

- Lower course creation friction.
- Improve instructional quality.
- Make source-backed teaching easier.
- Help educators design better assessments.

### Ecosystem impact

- Normalize proof over certificates.
- Raise quality standards for AI-assisted courses.
- Make free serious learning more accessible.

## Impact metrics

| Impact area | Metric |
|---|---|
| Access | Learners enrolled for free. |
| Engagement | Lesson completion and return rate. |
| Learning | Quiz improvement after feedback. |
| Practice | Practice and artifact completion. |
| Proof | Portfolio items created/shared. |
| Trust | Source drawer open and source trust ratings. |
| Tutor value | Tutor helpfulness and hallucination reports. |
| Educator quality | QA pass rate and revision cycles. |

## Social externalities to watch

Positive:

- More learners can access serious courses.
- Learners with low confidence can ask questions privately.
- Educators can teach without expensive production teams.
- Proof becomes more democratic.

Negative:

- Learners may over-rely on AI.
- AI may produce false confidence.
- Poor courses may scale if QA fails.
- BYOK may confuse or exclude some learners.
- Artifacts may be fabricated with AI.

## Impact guardrail

OpenEd should not optimize only for completion. Completion can be gamed. The deeper metric is:

```text
Learner produces a meaningful artifact and can explain it.
```

That is the beginning of proof.
